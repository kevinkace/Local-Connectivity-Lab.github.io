Overview

Through our volunteer efforts and community partnerships, we strive to provide low-income and in-need users with free or low-cost Internet service as reliably as possible. Our service is best-effort and will not necessarily match the performance of paid or commercial Internet service.

Our network includes site locations throughout King County and Tacoma, where we are able to provide coverage within specific neighborhoods near the sites. Please refer to the list of locations below to see if your neighborhood is covered.

Within these coverage areas, we can offer either home-based WiFi or mobile (phone-based) Internet access options.

Details

You will need to register with us and receive our SIM card to connect to the network. You may also need to receive a compatible mobile phone or stationary home hotspot from us that supports our wireless frequencies.

We expect that speeds would typically range from 30-90 Mbps download/2-14 Mbps upload. Part of the purpose of this pilot project is to determine the technology’s real performance and suitability for our use case.

The speed of your service will depend roughly on:

  • how many other users are currently using the network
  • how close your location is to the access point
  • whether you are using the mobile phone-based or home-based service (home-based is usually faster)

Eligibility

For the first round of our installations, which have been funded this past year by King County and the City of Seattle IT, we will prioritize serving the following groups:

  • low-income families of students
  • unemployed adults (looking for work)
  • majority non-English speaking adults/families
  • seniors

If you are eligible and interested in participating, please email lcl@seattlecommunitynetwork.org or leave a voice message or text at (253) 655-7221. You may also drop in at our in-person Technology Help Desk Hours (details <a href='https://docs.seattlecommunitynetwork.org/community/tech-help'>here</a>).
